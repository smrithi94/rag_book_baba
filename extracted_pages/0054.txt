The Double Halo
Baba was at the Dharmakshetra in Bombay. It is built on a small 
hill and includes, among other structures, a medium-sized building 
which serves to house some of the devotees in Baba's party while 
he is there. In addition to sleeping rooms, there is a kitchen and a 
dining room. The front area of the structure has two banks of 
sleeping rooms divided by a central plaza. The ladies of the party 
use one bank of rooms, and usually there is just a curtain across 
the doorways except during sleeping hours. The men have the 
same situation in the opposite bank of rooms. As is well known, 
Baba is almost invariably in a light-hearted mood, and he freely 
enters both sections of the building talking to, and joking with his 
devotees. His private quarters are in the principal building, some 
hundreds of feet away.
